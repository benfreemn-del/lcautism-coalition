Our Story

From a Single Gathering to a Statewide Movement

Inspired by Dr. Isaac Pope of Pope's Kids Place, the Lewis County Autism Coalition was founded in 2010 through a state-led Community Asset Mapping Project. From that first gathering of parents and providers, a movement took root -- grounded in collaboration, inclusion, and action. Now a longstanding 501(c)(3) nonprofit, LCAC continues to champion belonging for autistic, neurodiverse, and developmentally disabled individuals across Washington State.

Milestones

Key Achievements

Every milestone reflects our community's commitment to building a more inclusive world, one step at a time.

🧵

Weighted Warmth Program

Providing free compression and weighted vests to individuals who benefit from deep-pressure sensory input -- removing cost as a barrier to comfort.

💰

Micro-Grant Program

Supporting schools and community organizations with targeted funding to advance autism-friendly and inclusive initiatives at the local level.

🚗

2nd Annual Car & BMX Show (2025)

A community-wide celebration featuring cars, BMX, and family fun -- raising awareness and support while bringing people together in an inclusive setting.

🏠

Spectrum & Development Community Center

The grand opening of a 5,000 sq ft community-built space in Napavine -- a home for programs, services, and connection, co-located with Lewis County Pediatrics & Family Medicine.

Our Mission

The Lewis County Autism Coalition remains committed to building a more inclusive future for autistic, neurodiverse, and developmentally disabled individuals -- locally and beyond. Rooted in strong partnerships and grassroots leadership, we work every day to ensure that everyone has the opportunity to belong, grow, and thrive.
